Problem 4 ​

If the ratio of periods of two signals is not a rational number, then their sum is not periodic. When the ratio of periods of two signals is a rational number, the period of their sum is the least common multiple of their periods.

If the ratio of the periods of two signals is a rational number, then their product is periodic and the period is less than or equal to their least common multiple. If the ratio of their periods is not a rational number, then their product is not periodic.

function y = diffeqn(a,x,yn1)
    y = [];
    n = size(x);
    n = n(1,2);
    for i = 1:n
        if i ~= 1
            y(i) = a*y(i-1)+x(i);
        else 
            y(i) = a*yn1+x(i);
        end
    end
end
